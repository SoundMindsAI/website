Hamster Care Q&A

Hamsters should primarily eat a diet of commercial hamster food pellets. Additionally, they can have fresh vegetables, fruits (in moderation), and occasional protein sources like boiled egg or mealworms.

It's important to clean the hamster cage at least once a week. Spot clean daily by removing any soiled bedding and uneaten food. Use a pet-safe disinfectant for thorough cleaning.

Syrian hamsters are solitary and should be housed alone. Dwarf hamsters can sometimes live in pairs or small groups if they are introduced at a young age and monitored closely for any signs of aggression.

Handle your hamster gently and calmly. Let them get used to your scent by offering treats and allowing them to climb onto your hand voluntarily. Support their body and avoid sudden movements.

Signs of illness in hamsters include loss of appetite, lethargy, hunched posture, discharge from eyes or nose, and changes in fur appearance. Contact a veterinarian experienced with small pets if you notice any of these symptoms.
